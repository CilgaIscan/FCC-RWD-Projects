Barbara McClintock

The mother of jumping genes


Barbara McClintock, shown with her microscope.


Here's information about Barbara McClintock's life:

  • 1902 - Born in Connecticut, US.
  • 1919 - Started Cornell's College of Agriculture.
  • 1923 - Received BSc in Botany.
  • 1925 & 1927 - Gained her MSc and PhD degrees respectively in Plant Breeding Science in Cornell University..
  • 1931 - Publishes (with H. Creighton) article on genetic material crossing-over from one chromosome to another in maize.
  • 1931 - Fellow, National Research Council; conducts research at Cornell, University of Missouri at Columbia, and California Institute of Technology; discovers ring chromosomes and nucleolar organizer region; names the breakage-fusion-bridge cycle.
  • 1944 - Starts research on mobile genes and controlling elements.
  • 1970 - Receives National Medal of Science (first woman).
  • 1983 - Receives Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
  • 1992 - Dies September 2 in Huntington, New York.
